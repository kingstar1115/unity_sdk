noun	A word that functions as the name of some specific thing or set of things; can be the main word of a sentence's subject or the object of a verb or preposition
verb	A word that can be conjugated; forms the main part of the predicate of a sentence
adj	A word or phrase naming an attribute, added to or grammatically related to a noun to modify or describe it
adv	A word or phrase that modifies or qualifies an adjective, verb, or other adverb
det	A modifying words that determines the kind of reference a noun or noun group has, like 'the' or 'a'
prep	A word governing, and usually precedeing, a noun or pronoun and expressing a relation to another word in the clause. Examples are 'in', 'about', 'at'
subconj	Subordinate conjunction, like 'if', 'after'
conj	Coordinate conjunction, like 'and', 'or'...
qual	Qualifier, like 'very', 'even'. Can modify adverbs and adjectives, not verbs
subinf	For multiwords like 'in order to' and 'so as to' that act like the preinfinitive 'to' for infinitive verbs
infto	The preinfinitive sense of 'to', or analogs in other languages
forto	The sense of 'for' in 'for-to' constructions like 'for JJ to be there'
thatconj	The subordinate conjunction sense of 'that'
special	A catch-all category for special "words" like apostrophe-s
incomplete	Used as the part-of-speech category for the top node of an parsed deemed "incomplete"
